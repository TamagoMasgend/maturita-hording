# 18 - USA

## 1. Basic information
The United States of America (USA) is a federal republic consisting of 50 states and a federal district (Washington, D.C.). It is located in North America. The official language is English (though Spanish is widely spoken). The currency is the US Dollar (USD), and the population is approximately 330 million people. It is currently the world's foremost economic and military power.

## 2. Geography
- **Mountains:** The western part is dominated by the **Rocky Mountains** (The Rockies) and the Sierra Nevada. The highest peak in the USA is **Denali** (Mount McKinley) in Alaska. In the east, there are the older, lower **Appalachian Mountains**.
- **Lakes:** Dominated by the **Great Lakes** on the border with Canada: Lake Superior, Lake Michigan (entirely within the US), Lake Huron, Lake Erie, and Lake Ontario.
- **Rivers:** The most important river system is the **Mississippi River** and its main tributary, the **Missouri River**. In the west, the Colorado River carved the Grand Canyon.
- **Climate/Parts:** 
  - *Northeast:* Temperate continental climate.
  - *South/Southeast:* Humid subtropical climate (prone to hurricanes).
  - *Midwest:* Huge flat lowlands (The Great Plains) used for agriculture, prone to tornadoes (Tornado Alley).
  - *Southwest:* Desert climate (Death Valley in California is the hottest and driest place).
  - *West Coast:* Oceanic and Mediterranean climate.

## 3. People
The USA is often described as a "melting pot" of cultures. Native Americans form a very small minority today. Most people are descendants of European immigrants (British, Irish, German, Italian). There is a large African American population (descendants of slaves), a rapidly growing Hispanic population (from Mexico and Latin America), and Asian Americans. 

## 4. History
- **1492:** Christopher Columbus discovered America.
- **1776:** The original 13 British colonies signed the **Declaration of Independence** on July 4th, starting the Revolutionary War against Great Britain, which they won under George Washington.
- **1861–1865: The American Civil War.** The industrialized North fought the agricultural South over the issues of states' rights and slavery. The North (led by Abraham Lincoln) won, and slavery was abolished.
- **20th Century:** The US emerged victorious from both World Wars. Engaged in the Cold War against the Soviet Union.

## 5. Industry
The USA has the largest economy in the world. It is a leader in technological innovation (Silicon Valley in California for computers and software), aerospace (NASA, Boeing), entertainment (Hollywood for movies), telecommunications, and finance (Wall Street). They also produce massive amounts of oil, natural gas, and agricultural products (corn, wheat).

## 6. Interesting places
- **3 Cities:**
  1. *New York City:* The largest city, famous for the Statue of Liberty, Times Square, and Wall Street.
  2. *Los Angeles:* Located in California, the heart of the global entertainment industry (Hollywood, Walk of Fame).
  3. *Las Vegas:* Placed in the Nevada desert, famous worldwide for its mega-casino hotels, nightlife, and entertainment.
- **3 National Parks:**
  1. *Yellowstone:* The world's first national park, primarily in Wyoming. Famous for its geothermal features (Old Faithful geyser) and wildlife (bears, bison).
  2. *Grand Canyon:* In Arizona, a massive, immense red-rock canyon carved by the Colorado River over millions of years.
  3. *Yosemite:* In California, famous for its spectacular granite cliffs (El Capitan), giant sequoia trees, and huge waterfalls.

## 7. Government administration
The USA is a federal republic. The government is divided into three branches: Executive (The President), Legislative (The Congress: Senate + House of Representatives), and Judicial (The Supreme Court). The current President resides in the White House in Washington D.C.
- **Largest state:** Alaska.
- **Smallest state:** Rhode Island.
- **5 Largest cities (by population):** 
  1. New York City, 2. Los Angeles, 3. Chicago, 4. Houston, 5. Phoenix.
