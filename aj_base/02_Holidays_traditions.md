# 02 - Holidays, traditions

## 1) Typical Czech holidays

1. **St. Wenceslas Day (September 28):** Czech Statehood Day, honoring the patron saint of Bohemia.
2. **Independent Czechoslovak State Day (October 28):** Commemorates the creation of Czechoslovakia in 1918.
3. **Struggle for Freedom and Democracy Day (November 17):** Remembers student protests against Nazis (1939) and the Velvet Revolution (1989).
4. **Easter Monday (Velikonoční pondělí):** Boys whip girls with a braided willow stick ("pomlázka") for youth and health, receiving painted eggs or sweets.
5. **St. Nicholas Day (December 5th evening):** Mikuláš, an angel, and a devil visit children, giving sweets to the good ones and coal/potatoes to the bad ones.

## 2) Typical British holidays

1. **Guy Fawkes Night (Bonfire Night - November 5):** Commemorates the failed Gunpowder Plot of 1605 (attempt to blow up the Parliament). Celebrated with bonfires and fireworks.
2. **St. Patrick’s Day (March 17):** Patron saint of Ireland. People wear green, attend parades, and drink Guinness.
3. **Boxing Day (December 26):** Originally a day to give boxes of gifts to servants. Today, it is a major shopping holiday with big sales.

## 3) Typical American holidays

1. **Independence Day (July 4):** Commemorates the Declaration of Independence in 1776. Celebrated with fireworks, barbecues, and family picnics.
2. **Thanksgiving (4th Thursday in November):** Details below.
3. **Memorial Day (Last Monday in May):** Honors military personnel who have died in the performance of their military duties.

## 4) Other English Speaking Countries holidays

1. **Canada Day (July 1 - Canada):** Celebrates the anniversary of the Canadian Confederation in 1867.
2. **Waitangi Day (February 6 - New Zealand):** The national day of NZ, marking the signing of the Treaty of Waitangi in 1840.
3. **Australia Day (January 26 - Australia):** Marks the arrival of the First Fleet of British ships in 1788.

## 5) Easter

- **When:** A movable feast, usually in March or April (the first Sunday after the first full moon in spring).
- **Where:** Celebrated worldwide by Christians.
- **Why / Origin:** Celebrates the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead. Originally rooted in pagan festivals celebrating the arrival of spring.
- **Symbols:** Easter bunny, painted eggs, lambs, spring flowers (daffodils).
- **Traditions in the UK/USA:** Egg hunts for children (looking for hidden chocolate eggs), eating Hot Cross Buns (sweet spiced buns with a cross).

## 6) Halloween

- **Details:** Celebrated on October 31st. Originally a Celtic festival (Samhain) marking the end of the harvest. People believed ghosts returned to Earth.
- **Traditions:** "Trick-or-treating" (children wear costumes and ask for sweets), carving pumpkins into Jack-o'-lanterns, wearing scary costumes, watching horror movies.

## 7) Thanksgiving

- **Details:** Celebrated on the fourth Thursday in November in the USA.
- **Origin:** Commemorates a 1621 harvest feast shared by the English colonists (Pilgrims) and the Wampanoag Native Americans.
- **Traditions:** Families gather for a massive feast. The traditional meal is roast turkey, stuffing, cranberry sauce, mashed potatoes, and pumpkin pie.

## 8) St. Valentine

- **Details:** Celebrated on February 14th. It is the festival of lovers.
- **Origin:** The holiday is named after **Saint Valentine**, a Christian priest in Rome.
  Emperor Claudius II banned marriages for young soldiers, believing unmarried men made better warriors. Secretly, performed marriages for young couples. Executed and declared a saint.
- **Traditions:** People express their love by secretly sending Valentine's cards, giving red roses, chocolates in heart-shaped boxes, or going for romantic dinners.

## Christmas in the UK

**Symbols:** Christmas tree, Christmas wreath (hung on front door), mistletoe (kiss under it), stockings (hung by fireplace), candy canes, Christmas crackers (paper tubes that pop — contain a joke, paper crown, small toy), star or angel on top of tree.

1. **Advent:** In England, Christmas starts with Advent. During this time, people decorate their houses, buy presents, and prepare the Christmas pudding. It is a special dense cake full of nuts, raisins, and dried fruit.
2. **December 23rd:** This is typically an ordinary working day, usually the last day of frantic shopping or traveling.
3. **Christmas Eve (December 24th):** They decorate the Christmas tree and put a fairy or a star on the top. (Unlike in the CZ, the main celebration is NOT on this day).
4. **Christmas Day (December 25th):** Children get up very early to see what Father Christmas left in their stockings. The family gathers by the tree and opens presents.
   - They have a traditional lunch consisting of roast turkey with chestnut stuffing, roast potatoes, Brussels sprouts, and gravy.
   - The dessert is the traditional Christmas pudding (often set on fire with brandy before serving).
5. **Boxing Day (December 26th):** People relax, visit relatives, or go shopping because the massive post-Christmas discounts (sales) start.
6. **New Year's Eve (December 31st):** Celebrated similarly to the Czech Republic – fireworks, parties, and the countdown to midnight (often linked to the chiming of Big Ben).
