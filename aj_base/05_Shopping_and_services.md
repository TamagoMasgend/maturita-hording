# 05 - Shopping and services

## Shopping

### Shops in my town
In my town, there is a variety of shops. There are several large supermarkets (like Kaufland or Albert) where people buy their daily groceries. In the town square, there are specialized shops such as bakeries, butcher shops, pharmacies, and small clothing stores. On the outskirts, there is a large shopping mall with international fashion brands, electronics, and a food court.

### My favourite and least favourite shops
**Favourite:** My favourite shops are bookstores and electronics shops (like Datart or Alza). I like browsing through new books or checking out the latest laptop and phone technologies.
**Least favourite:** My least favourite shops are large clothing stores and shoe shops. Trying on clothes is exhausting for me, and the shops are often overcrowded and noisy.

### How often do you shop and everywhere?
I personally do not shop very often. I usually buy a snack or a drink on my way to school. My parents do the main grocery shopping once a week on Friday afternoon, usually driving to a hypermarket because it is cheaper and they can buy items in bulk. I only go clothes shopping when I absolutely need something, about two or three times a year.

### Internet shopping
**Advantages:**
- Immense convenience: you can shop 24/7 from the comfort of your home.
- You can easily compare prices across different stores using comparison websites (e.g., Heureka).
- Wider variety of products, and items are often cheaper without the overhead of a physical store.
**Disadvantages:**
- You cannot see, touch, or try on the product before buying it (which is problematic for clothes or shoes).
- You have to wait for delivery and pay for shipping.
- Risk of scams or stolen credit card info.
**Your experience:** I frequently use online shopping for electronics and video games. I have always had a good experience, the packages arrive quickly, and returning items if they are damaged is usually easy.

## Services

### 1. Rozdělení + služby
Services are generally divided into two main categories: **Public services** and **Private services**. Both exist to make everyday life easier and meet the needs of the population.

### 2. Public
Public services are usually provided or heavily subsidized by the government or local municipality and are funded by taxes. They are designed to benefit society as a whole.
Examples of public services we use include:
- **Public Transport:** Buses, trams, and trains.
- **Healthcare:** Public hospitals and emergency services.
- **Education:** State schools and universities.
- **Safety:** Police, fire brigade, and military.
- **Infrastructure:** Maintenance of parks, roads, and street lighting.
- **Libraries and Public pools.**

### 3. Private
Private services are run by businesses aiming to make a profit. Individuals pay directly for these services.
Examples include:
- **Beauty services:** Hairdressers, barbers, nail salons, and spas.
- **Maintenance/Repair:** Plumbers, electricians, car mechanics.
- **Consultation:** Financial advisors, private lawyers, real estate agents.
- **Cleaning:** Dry cleaners, laundromats, or home cleaning services.
- **Fitness:** Private gyms and personal trainers.

### 4. Post office
At a post office, you can:
- Send and receive letters, postcards, and parcels.
- Buy stamps, envelopes, and packaging materials.
- Pay utility bills (electricity, water) or cash a postal order (složenka).
- Pick up registered mail (doporučený dopis) that requires an ID and signature.

### 5. Bank
At a bank, you can:
- Open or close a bank account (current account or savings account).
- Withdraw or deposit cash.
- Ask for a loan or a mortgage to buy an apartment or house.
- Exchange foreign currencies.
- Speak with a financial advisor about investments.

### 6. Restaurace a ubytování (Restaurants and Accommodation)
- **Restaurants:** Offer dining services where you can order food and drinks from a menu. Services range from fast food to formal dining. Waiters serve you at the table, and you are expected to leave a tip for good service.
- **Accommodation:** Includes hotels, hostels, and motels. They provide a place to sleep, often with additional services like breakfast, room service, or a pool. Guests must make a reservation and check-in at the reception.
