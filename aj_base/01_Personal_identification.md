# 01 - Personal Identification

## Is family important?
Yes, family is essential because it provides emotional support, a sense of belonging, and a foundation for personal development. It offers financial security during childhood and shapes an individual's moral values and behavior.

## Do you think children and parents can become friends as adults?
Yes, the relationship between parents and children usually evolves. As children grow up and become independent, the dynamic shifts from authoritative to mutual respect. They can share hobbies, discuss problems as equals, and enjoy each other's company as friends.

## When should parents start treating their kids as adults? 
Parents should gradually start treating their kids as adults during late adolescence (around 16–18 years old). Important milestones include giving them responsibility for their finances, educational choices, and trusting them to face the consequences of their actions.

## Would you like to have a sibling? Why yes why not?
*(Personal choice example)* Yes, having a sibling means having a life-long companion. Siblings teach us how to share, cooperate, and resolve conflicts. Alternatively, being an only child means receiving undivided parental attention and resources.

## Would you like to have a family?
*(Personal choice example)* Yes, I would like to have a family in the future because I want to raise children, pass down knowledge, and build a supportive environment. The stability of home life is a long-term goal.

## How important is it to have privacy at home?
Having privacy is crucial for mental health. Everyone needs personal space to relax, focus on hobbies, study without interruption, and process their thoughts. Lack of privacy can lead to stress and family conflicts.

## Advantages and disadvantages of living in a multigenerational house?
**Advantages:** 
- Shared financial costs (rent, utilities).
- Grandparents can help with childcare.
- Stronger family bonds and care for the elderly.
**Disadvantages:**
- Lack of privacy for individual families.
- Generation gap can lead to arguments over lifestyle and parenting methods.

## Where would you like to live in the next few years?
*(Personal choice example)* I would like to live in a city to have better access to university education, career opportunities, and cultural events. A smaller apartment near the center would be ideal for commuting.

## What housework do you like and dislike?

**Dislike:** Ironing and washing dishes, as they are repetitive and time-consuming.

## My daily routine

### 1. Monday to Friday mornings
I usually wake up at 6:30 AM. I go to the bathroom, brush my teeth, and get dressed. I have a quick breakfast, usually cereal or toast with tea, prepare my snack, and leave the house around 7:15 AM to catch the bus.

### 2. School time
School starts at 8:00 AM. A typical day has 6 to 8 lessons. 
- **Favourite breaks:** The 20-minute break at 9:40 AM is my favorite as I eat my snack and chat with friends. 
- **Lunch / Brunch:** We have lunch in the school canteen, usually around 12:30 or 1:30 PM depending on the schedule.
- **School finishes at:** Usually between 2:00 PM and 3:30 PM.

### 3. Afternoon
After school ("konec školy"), I take the bus home. I usually relax for an hour, listen to music or browse the internet. Then I do my homework and study for the next day. Sometimes I have extracurricular activities like language courses or sports practice. Dinner is usually around 7:00 PM with the whole family.

### 4. Evenings
After dinner, I help with the dishes, take a shower, and pack my backpack for the next day. I spend the rest of the evening watching a TV series, reading a book, or chatting with friends online. I usually go to sleep at 11:00 PM.

### 5. Weekends
- **Mornings:** I sleep in until 9:00 or 10:00 AM, have a slow, big breakfast.
- **Afternoons:** I spend time on my hobbies, play PC games, go out with friends, or help with bigger household chores (cleaning, gardening).
- **Evenings:** Often spent watching a movie with family or socializing. I go to sleep later, around midnight or 1:00 AM.
