# 15 - The United Kingdom

## 1) Basic information

The official name is the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (UK). It is an island nation located off the northwestern coast of mainland Europe. It consists of four countries: England (capital: London), Scotland (capital: Edinburgh), Wales (capital: Cardiff), and Northern Ireland (capital: Belfast). The official language is English, and the currency is the Pound Sterling (GBP). The population is around 67 million.

## 2) Geography

- **Mountains:** The UK is generally hilly in the north and west. The highest mountain is **Ben Nevis** (1,345m) in the Grampian Mountains in Scotland. Other ranges include the Pennines (the "backbone of England") and the Cambrian Mountains in Wales.
- **Lakes:** Known as lochs in Scotland and loughs in Northern Ireland. The most famous is **Loch Ness** (famous for its monster legend). The largest by area is Lough Neagh in Northern Ireland. In England, the Lake District is highly famous (Windermere).
- **Rivers:** The longest river is the **River Severn** (354 km). The most famous and important river is the **River Thames**, which flows through London.
- **Parts/Regions:** The UK is surrounded by the Atlantic Ocean, the North Sea, the English Channel (which separates it from France), and the Irish Sea. Geographically, southeast England is mostly lowlands, while Scotland and Wales are highly mountainous and rugged.

## 3) People

The UK has a highly diverse and multicultural population, especially in large cities like London, Birmingham, and Manchester, due to its colonial history and modern immigration from India, Pakistan, the Caribbean, and Eastern Europe. The native people are a mix of Celtic, Roman, Anglo-Saxon, Viking, and Norman heritage.

## 4) History

- **Early History:** Originally inhabited by Celts, later conquered by the Romans (who built Hadrian's Wall). Then came the Anglo-Saxons and Vikings.
- **1066:** The Norman Conquest led by William the Conqueror (Battle of Hastings), which heavily influenced the English language and culture.
- **The Wars of the Roses (1455–1487):** A series of civil wars between the House of Lancaster (red rose) and the House of York (white rose) for control of the English throne. The conflict ended when Henry Tudor (Lancaster) defeated Richard III at the Battle of Bosworth Field and became Henry VII, founding the Tudor dynasty and uniting the two houses.
- **19th Century:** The Victorian Era. The British Empire became the largest empire in world history, leading the global Industrial Revolution.
- **20th/21st Century:** Won both World Wars but lost its empire. Joined the EU in 1973 and officially left it in 2020 through "Brexit".

## 5) Industry

The UK was the birthplace of the Industrial Revolution (originally coal mining, steel production, and textiles). Today, heavy manufacturing has declined greatly. The UK economy is heavily dominated by the **service sector**, particularly financial services (The City of London is a global financial center). Furthermore, it has a strong aerospace industry, pharmaceutical industry (e.g., AstraZeneca), high-tech engineering, and tourism.

## 6) Government and administration

The UK is a **constitutional monarchy** with a **parliamentary democracy**.

- **The Monarch:** King Charles III is the head of state. The monarchy is mostly ceremonial with no real political power.
- **The Parliament:** Located in the Palace of Westminster, it makes the laws. It has two houses: the House of Commons (elected MPs) and the House of Lords (appointed members).
- **The Prime Minister:** The head of the government, usually the leader of the majority party in the House of Commons. The two dominating political parties are the Conservative Party and the Labour Party.

## 7) Interesting places

1. **Stonehenge:** A prehistoric circle of massive standing stones in Wiltshire, England. Its exact original purpose remains a mystery, likely a religious site or astronomical observatory.
2. **The Scottish Highlands:** Famous for stunning rugged landscapes, deep lochs, and historical castles.
3. **Stratford-upon-Avon:** A historic market town in England, world-famous as the birthplace of the playwright William Shakespeare.
4. **Giant's Causeway:** Located in Northern Ireland, it is an area of about 40,000 interlocking basalt columns, the result of an ancient volcanic fissure eruption.
