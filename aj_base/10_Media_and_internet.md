# 10 - Media and internet

## 1) Introduction
- **Definition of mass media and internet:** Mass media refers to diverse technologies that reach a large audience via mass communication (print, broadcast, or digital). The Internet is a global system of interconnected computer networks that links billions of devices worldwide.
- **Brief history and evolution:** Mass media started with the invention of the printing press (by Gutenberg) in the 15th century, leading to newspapers. The 20th century introduced radio and television. The late 20th and early 21st century revolutionized media with the creation of the World Wide Web, making the internet the dominant medium today.

## 2) Types of Mass Media
- **Print media (tisková média):** Newspapers (broadsheets - serious news like The Times; tabloids - gossip like The Sun), magazines, books, and billboards.
- **Electronic/Broadcast media (elektronická média):** Television (public and commercial channels) and Radio (music, news, podcasts).
- **Digital media (digitální média):** The Internet, social media platforms (Instagram, TikTok, X), blogs, and digital news portals.

## 3) The Role of Mass Media in Society
- **Informing the public:** Delivering daily news, weather forecasts, and providing updates on global and local political events.
- **Education and Entertainment:** Broadcasting documentaries, educational programs, movies, series, and talk shows to entertain people in their free time.
- **Shaping public opinion (formování veřejného mínění):** Media greatly influence how people think about politics, brands, and social issues by highlighting certain topics. In extreme cases, this can lead to propaganda.

## 4) The Internet and Its Impact
- **Quick access to information:** By typing a query into a search engine (like Google), people can instantly access millions of articles, Wikipedia pages, and tutorials globally.
- **Social networks and their influence:** Platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok connect people worldwide. They allow sharing of photos, messages, and opinions, heavily impacting pop culture, fast-fashion, and youth behaviour.
- **E-commerce and online services:** The internet has revolutionized businesses through online shopping (Amazon, Alza), internet banking, and streaming services (Netflix, Spotify).

## 5) Advantages and Disadvantages of Mass Media and the internet
- **Advantages:**
  - **Speed:** News is delivered almost instantly.
  - **Availability:** Access to information and entertainment 24/7 from anywhere via a smartphone.
  - **Interactivity:** Users can comment, share, and communicate directly instead of just passively consuming content.
- **Disadvantages:**
  - **Disinformation (Fake news):** False information spreads rapidly, confusing the public.
  - **Addiction:** High use of social media and the internet can lead to screen addiction and social isolation.
  - **Privacy:** Loss of privacy, cyberbullying, and tracking of personal data by big tech companies.

## 6) The Future of Mass Media and the internet
- **Trends and Innovations:** The rise of Artificial Intelligence (AI) generating articles, images, and videos. The transition from traditional TV to on-demand streaming and virtual reality (VR) experiences.
- **Challenges and Opportunities:** The main challenge will be verifying the truthfulness of information (stopping fake news and deepfakes) while maintaining freedom of speech. Opportunities lie in globally accessible, personalized education.

## 7) Conclusion
- **Summary:** Mass media and the internet have fundamentally evolved from paper to screens, shifting how society stays informed, works, and entertains itself.
