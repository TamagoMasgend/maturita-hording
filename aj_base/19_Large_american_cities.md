# 19 - Large American Cities

## 1. Washington, D.C.
- **Basic information:** It is the capital of the United States. "D.C." stands for District of Columbia. It is not part of any U.S. state, but an independent federal district on the East Coast (Potomac River).
- **History:** Founded after the American Revolution directly as the seat of government to ensure no single state had control over the capital. It was planned and designed from scratch by Pierre Charles L'Enfant.
- **Interesting places & details:**
  - *The White House:* The official residence and workplace of the President of the United States. It has 132 rooms, including the famous Oval Office.
  - *The Capitol:* The home of the United States Congress and the seat of the legislative branch. It sits atop Capitol Hill and has a massive iconic white dome.
  - *The National Mall:* A large open park area containing numerous iconic monuments (The Washington Monument obelisk, the Lincoln Memorial) and world-class free museums (Smithsonian Institution).

## 2. New York City
- **Boroughs:** New York is composed of 5 distinct boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island.
- **The Manhattan skyline:** It is globally famous for its dense concentration of massive skyscrapers. Notable buildings include the Empire State Building, the Chrysler Building, and One World Trade Center (which replaced the Twin Towers destroyed on 9/11).
- **Streets & Avenues:** Manhattan is built on a grid plan. *Avenues* run North-South (e.g., 5th Avenue, famous for luxury shopping) and *Streets* run East-West (e.g., Wall Street, the financial centre; or Broadway, running diagonally, famous for its theatre district).
- **3 other interesting places:**
  1. *The Statue of Liberty:* A colossal copper statue on Liberty Island, gifted by France in 1886. A symbol of freedom and welcome to immigrants.
  2. *Central Park:* A massive urban park in the middle of Manhattan providing a green oasis with lakes, a zoo, and walking paths.
  3. *Times Square:* A major commercial intersection heavily illuminated by massive digital billboards, known as "The Crossroads of the World."

## 3. Los Angeles
- **Basic information:** Located in southern California on the Pacific coast. It is the second-largest city in the USA. The city is sprawling and heavily dependant on cars and freeways.
- **Interesting places:**
  - *Hollywood:* The global centre of the film and entertainment industry. 
  - *The Hollywood Walk of Fame:* Sidewalks embedded with brass stars honoring celebrities in the entertainment industry.
  - *Beverly Hills & Rodeo Drive:* A very wealthy area where many movie stars live, featuring ultra-luxury shopping on Rodeo Drive.
  - *Venice Beach / Santa Monica:* Famous beaches with a carnival-like boardwalk and the historic Santa Monica Pier.

## 4. Chicago
- **Basic information:** Located in the state of Illinois in the Midwest, exactly on the shore of Lake Michigan. It is the third-largest city in the USA, often nicknamed "The Windy City."
- **Interesting places:**
  - *Willis Tower (formerly Sears Tower):* One of the tallest buildings in the world. Its Skydeck features glass boxes extending out from the building, offering terrifying views straight down.
  - *Millennium Park:* Contains the famous "Cloud Gate" steel sculpture (often called "The Bean") which reflects the city's skyline.
  - *Navy Pier:* A long pier extending into Lake Michigan, offering entertainment, rides (a giant Ferris wheel), restaurants, and exhibitions.
