# 19 - Large American Cities

## 1. Washington, D.C.

- **Basic information:** It is the capital of the United States, named after the 1st president George Washington. "D.C." stands for District of Columbia — it is not part of any U.S. state, but an independent federal district and the seat of the federal government. It was the first city in the world especially built as a capital. The population is around 623 thousand (about 4 million with the conurbation).
- **History:** Founded in 1790 and became the capital in 1800. It was planned and designed from scratch by Pierre Charles L'Enfant to ensure no single state had control over the capital.
- **Interesting places & details:**
  - *The White House:* The official residence and workplace of the President. George Washington started the idea of building it, but the 2nd President John Adams was the first to actually live there. It has 132 rooms, including the famous Oval Office.
  - *The Capitol:* The home of the United States Congress and the seat of the legislative branch. It is the highest building in Washington, D.C. (no building is allowed to be taller). It sits atop Capitol Hill and has a massive iconic white dome. Washington is also the seat of many other institutions like the Pentagon.
  - *The Washington Monument:* A column (obelisk) 555 feet high — visitors can go to the top by lift.
  - *Washington National Cathedral:* Placed on the highest point of Washington (Mt. St. Alban), built in Gothic style. It is the Cathedral of St. Peter and St. Paul.
  - *The National Mall:* A large open park area containing numerous iconic monuments (the Lincoln Memorial) and world-class free museums (Smithsonian Institution).

## 2. New York City

- **Basic information:** New York is the biggest city in the USA. The central area has about 8 million inhabitants but the whole metropolitan area has 18 million. The population density is one of the highest in the world — about 9,200 people per 1 sq km. New York lies on the east coast at the mouth of the Hudson and East Rivers; on the East River there are important industrial ports.
- **Boroughs:** New York is composed of 5 distinct boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island (Richmond).
- **The Manhattan skyline:** A large number of skyscrapers packed into a small area. Notable buildings include the Empire State Building (built in 1931, 381 m high), the Chrysler Building, and One World Trade Center (which replaced the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center, destroyed on 9/11).
- **Streets & Avenues:** The oldest streets have names — Wall Street (the symbol of the financial world), Broadway (famous for its theatre district). The rest follow a grid plan: *Streets* run East-West and *Avenues* run North-South (e.g., 5th Avenue, famous for luxury shopping).
- **3 other interesting places:**
  1. *The Statue of Liberty:* A colossal copper statue on Liberty Island, gifted by France in 1886. A symbol of freedom and of the US itself.
  2. *Central Park:* A massive urban park in the middle of Manhattan providing a green oasis with lakes, a zoo, and walking paths.
  3. *Times Square:* A major commercial intersection heavily illuminated by massive digital billboards, known as "The Crossroads of the World."

## 3. Los Angeles

- **Basic information:** Located in southern California on the Pacific coast. It is the second-largest city in the USA with 3.5 million inhabitants (10 million with the conurbation). Its name comes from Spanish for "the City of Angels", because it originally belonged to the Spanish territory of Mexico. The city is sprawling and heavily dependent on cars and freeways.
- **Interesting places:**
  - *Hollywood:* The global centre of the film and entertainment industry, making LA an important tourist attraction.
  - *The Hollywood Walk of Fame:* Sidewalks embedded with brass stars honoring celebrities in the entertainment industry.
  - *Beverly Hills:* The most famous suburb for celebrities and interesting sites related to movies.
  - *Disneyland:* The original Disneyland theme park is located nearby in Anaheim.

## 4. Chicago

- **Basic information:** Located in the state of Illinois in the Midwest, on the shore of Lake Michigan. It is the third-largest city in the USA, often nicknamed "The Windy City." There is the so-called St. Lawrence Seaway — a system of canals connecting the city with the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf of Mexico.
- **Interesting places:**
  - *Willis Tower (formerly Sears Tower):* 443 meters tall, it was the tallest building in the world for many years. Its Skydeck features glass boxes extending out from the building, offering terrifying views straight down.
  - *O'Hare International Airport:* One of the busiest airports in the world.
  - *Millennium Park:* Contains the famous "Cloud Gate" steel sculpture (often called "The Bean") which reflects the city's skyline.

## 5. San Francisco

- **Basic information:** A hilly city located in northern California on a peninsula between the Pacific Ocean and San Francisco Bay.
- **Interesting places:**
  - *Golden Gate Bridge:* Opened in 1937, it was the longest bridge in the world until 1964. It is still one of the highest suspension bridges in the world — each of its steel towers is 227 m high.
  - *Alcatraz Island:* Located in San Francisco Bay, it housed the infamous Alcatraz prison — escaping from it was practically impossible. Today it is part of the Golden Gate National Recreational Area and a popular tourist attraction.
