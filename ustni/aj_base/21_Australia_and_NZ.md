## Australia

### 1) Basic info
Australia is both a country and the world's smallest continent. It is located in Oceania between the Indian and Pacific oceans. The capital is **Canberra** (not Sydney). The official language is English, and the currency is the Australian Dollar (AUD). The population is about 26 million.

### 2) Geography
- **Mountains:** The most prominent mountain range is the **Great Dividing Range** in the east. The highest peak is **Mount Kosciuszko** (2,228m).
- **Lakes:** Most lakes in Australia are dry salt lakes. The largest is **Lake Eyre**, which only fills with water exceptionally rarely.
- **Rivers:** The most significant river system is the **Murray-Darling** basin in the southeast.
- **Parts/Regions:** The vast majority of the interior and west is extremely dry desert or semi-arid land known as the **Outback**. Due to this, over 80% of the population lives in coastal areas (mainly in the east and southeast), where the climate is temperate or tropical. Off the northeast coast lies the massive **Great Barrier Reef**.

### 3) People
Before British colonization, the land was inhabited by Indigenous Australians (Aborigines) for tens of thousands of years. Today, they form a small minority. The modern population is primarily of British and Irish descent, though post-WWII immigration has brought many people from Europe, and recently from Asia, making it a multicultural society.

### 4) History
- For millennia, only Aborigines lived there.
- In 1770, British explorer Captain **James Cook** mapped the eastern coast and claimed it for Great Britain.
- In 1788, the British established the first settlement in Sydney. Initially, it was used entirely as a **penal colony** (a prison for British convicts).
- During the 19th century, free settlers arrived (spurred by a gold rush).
- In 1901, the six separate British colonies federated to form the Commonwealth of Australia.

### 5) Industry
Australia is extremely wealthy in natural resources. Its economy relies heavily on **mining** (the world's largest exporter of iron ore, coal, and gold). **Agriculture** is also vital (huge exporter of wheat, beef, and sheep wool). Furthermore, **tourism** and international education are massive economic drivers.

### 6) Government
Australia is a federal parliamentary constitutional monarchy (King Charles III is the head of state). It is divided into 6 states (e.g., New South Wales, Queensland) and 2 major mainland territories. The federal government is run by the Prime Minister.

## New Zealand (NZ)

### 1) Basic info
New Zealand is an island country located southeast of Australia across the Tasman Sea. The capital is **Wellington**, and the largest city is Auckland. The population is relatively small (about 5 million). The official languages are English and Māori. The currency is the New Zealand Dollar (NZD).

### 2) Geography - Detailed
- **Of how many islands does it consist:** It mainly consists of two large islands: the **North Island** and the **South Island**, separated by the Cook Strait, plus hundreds of smaller islands (like Stewart Island).
- **Is it mountainous or lowland:** It is incredibly mountainous and volcanically active. The North Island features active volcanoes and hot springs (geothermal activity). The South Island is dominated by a massive mountain range called the **Southern Alps**, which has deep fjords and glaciers.
- **Highest Mountain:** The highest peak is **Aoraki / Mount Cook** (3,724m), located in the Southern Alps on the South Island.
- **Rivers:** Rivers are usually short, fast, and unnavigable, making them perfect for hydroelectric power. The longest river is the **Waikato River** on the North Island. The largest lake is **Lake Taupo**, which sits inside a volcanic caldera.

### 3) People
The original inhabitants are the **Māori**, a Polynesian people who arrived around 1300 AD. Today, Māori culture is a fundamental part of New Zealand's national identity. The majority of the modern population is of European descent (often called "Pākehā").

### 4) History
- Settlement began with the Māori.
- In 1642, Dutch explorer Abel Tasman was the first European to sight the islands (naming it after the Dutch province of Zeeland).
- In 1769, British Captain James Cook mapped the islands.
- In 1840, the British Crown and Māori chiefs signed the **Treaty of Waitangi**, establishing British sovereignty but guaranteeing Māori rights to their land. It became a fully independent nation over time, similarly to Australia and Canada.

### 5) Industry
Historically, the economy strongly depended on agriculture, especially sheep farming (New Zealand is famously known for having more sheep than people) and exporting dairy products, meat, and wool. Today, **tourism** is extremely important, driven by the country's spectacular natural scenery (famously used as the filming location for "The Lord of the Rings").

### 6) Government + large cities + map
- **Government:** A parliamentary representative democratic monarchy (King Charles III). Note: New Zealand was the first country in the world to give women the right to vote (in 1893).
- **Large cities:** **Auckland** (the largest commercial hub, located on the North Island), **Wellington** (the capital, North Island), **Christchurch** (South Island).
- **Map:** Positioned in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, long and narrow. The North Island is physically closer to the equator (warmer), while the South Island is closer to Antarctica (colder).
