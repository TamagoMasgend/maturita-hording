# 16 - London

## 1. Basic information

London is the capital and largest city of England and the United Kingdom. It is situated in the southeastern part of England on the River Thames. The population of Greater London is nearly 9 million. It is a global city, a major financial centre, and one of the most culturally diverse cities in the world.

## 2. History

London was founded by the Romans around 43 AD and was named _Londinium_. Throughout history, it survived several major catastrophes:

- **The Great Plague (1665):** Killed around 100,000 people (a quarter of the population).
- **The Great Fire of London (1666):** Destroyed the medieval wooden city, leading to the rebuilding of London in stone and brick (e.g., St. Paul's Cathedral designed by Sir Christopher Wren).
- **The Blitz (1940-1941):** Severe bombing by Nazi Germany during World War II, damaging large parts of the city.

## 3. Transport

London has a massive and complex transport system:

- **The London Underground (The Tube):** The oldest rapid transit (metro) system in the world, opened in 1863. It is recognizable by its red circle logo.
- **Double-decker buses:** Iconic red two-storey buses used for public transport.
- **Black Cabs:** Traditional London taxis driven by highly trained professionals who must pass a severe test called "The Knowledge."
- **Heathrow Airport:** One of the busiest airports in the world.
- **Railways:** Major stations include King's Cross, Victoria, and Waterloo.

## 4. Interesting places

- **The Tower of London:** A historic castle on the north bank of the Thames. It has served as a royal palace, a prison, and a place of execution. Today, it securely houses and displays the Crown Jewels.
- **Tower Bridge:** A famous combined bascule and suspension bridge crossing the River Thames, located close to the Tower of London.
- **The Houses of Parliament (Palace of Westminster):** The seat of the British government (House of Commons and House of Lords), famous for the Elizabeth Tower containing the Big Ben bell.
- **Westminster Abbey:** A large, historic Gothic church where British monarchs are crowned, married, and buried.
- **Whitehall:** A main street running south from Trafalgar Square, recognized as the center of the UK government with many ministries.
- **10 Downing Street:** A street branching off Whitehall, famous for being the official residence and office of the British Prime Minister.
- **Buckingham Palace:** The official residence of the British monarch in London, famous for the Changing of the Guard ceremony.
- **St. Paul’s Cathedral:** A magnificent Anglican cathedral designed by Sir Christopher Wren after the Great Fire of London, featuring a massive dome and the famous Whispering Gallery.
- **Trafalgar Square:** A major public square commemorating the Battle of Trafalgar, featuring Nelson's Column guarded by four giant bronze lions.
- **The British Museum:** A vast institution dedicated to human history, art, and culture, housing global artifacts including the Rosetta Stone and Egyptian mummies.
- **Victoria and Albert Museum (V&A):** The world's largest museum of applied and decorative arts, design, and sculpture.
- **Madame Tussauds:** A famous wax museum displaying lifelike wax figures of historical figures, celebrities, and sports stars.

## 5. Parks

London is one of the greenest cities in the world.

- **Hyde Park:** One of the largest Royal Parks, famous for its Speakers' Corner and the Serpentine lake.
- **Regent's Park:** Contains the London Zoo and an open-air theatre.
- **Greenwich Park:** A historic park offering views of the Thames, housing the Royal Observatory and the Prime Meridian Line.
- **Kew Gardens:** Royal Botanic Gardens housing the largest and most diverse botanical collections in the world.

## 6. Shopping

- **Oxford Street:** Europe's busiest shopping street, holding hundreds of flagship stores for mainstream fashion brands.
- **Regent Street:** Shopping street known for its high-end stores with Hamleys, the world's oldest toy shop.
- **Harrods:** Located in Knightsbridge, it is a massive, world-famous luxury department store known for its premium goods.
