# 03 - Travelling

## Reasons for travelling
1. **Leisure and Holidays:** To relax, escape the daily routine, and enjoy nice weather (going to the beach, skiing).
2. **Exploring and Culture:** Sightseeing, visiting historical monuments, museums, and learning about different cultures.
3. **Business:** Attending conferences, meetings, or negotiating trade deals.
4. **Education:** Studying abroad (e.g., Erasmus), learning foreign languages directly in the native country.

## Advantages and disadvantages of travelling
**Advantages:**
- Broadens knowledge and perspective on the world.
- Opportunity to meet new people and experience different cultures.
- Practicing foreign languages and building confidence.
**Disadvantages:**
- Can be very expensive.
- Can be stressful (delayed flights, lost luggage, language barriers).
- Environmental impact (high carbon footprint of air travel).

## Means of transportation
*12 types: Car, Bus, Train, Plane, Ferry/Ship, Bicycle, Motorcycle, Tram, Subway/Underground, Scooter, Helicopter, Cable car.*

**Target comparison: Plane, Train, Car**
- **Speed:** The fastest is the **plane** (ideal for long distances like intercontinental travel). The **train** can be fast (high-speed rail) but is slower than a plane. A **car** is the slowest among the three for long distances.
- **Comfort:** The **plane** is comfortable but spaces are cramped unless flying first class. The **train** offers great comfort, the ability to walk around, and enjoy the scenery. The **car** offers private comfort, where you can stop anytime you want.
- **Price:** The **plane** is generally the most expensive. The **train** is affordable, but heavily depends on the country. The **car** is cost-effective if traveling in a group (sharing gas/petrol).
- **Favourite:** My favourite is the train because it is eco-friendly, relaxing, and usually drops you off right in the city center.

## Needs for travelling
- **Documents:** ID card (within the EU/Schengen area) or a valid Passport. 
- **Visa:** An official stamp or document allowing entry into a country (e.g., USA requires an ESTA or tourist visa for some nations).
- **Other needs:** Travel insurance (covers medical expenses abroad), money (local currency or credit cards), tickets.

## Types of accommodation
*6 types: Hotel, Hostel, Bed & Breakfast (B&B), Campsite, Airbnb (Apartment rental), Motel.*

**Target comparison: Hotel vs. Hostel**
- **Service & Comfort:** A **hotel** provides high comfort, private bathrooms, daily cleaning, and reception services. A **hostel** provides low comfort, often shared dormitory rooms with bunk beds, and shared bathrooms.
- **Price:** A **hotel** is expensive, while a **hostel** is very cheap and budget-friendly, especially for young backpackers.
- **Favourite:** My favourite is the hotel because I prefer having my own privacy and comfort after a long day of sightseeing.

## Luggage
1. **Suitcase:** Hard-shell or soft material, with wheels, for longer stays.
2. **Backpack / Rucksack:** Ideal for hiking or when you need your hands free.
3. **Carry-on / Hand luggage:** A small bag or suitcase allowed directly into the plane cabin.
4. **Holdall / Duffel bag:** Cylindrical bag used often for sports or short trips.
5. **Briefcase:** Used mainly for business trips to carry documents and a laptop.
6. **Bumbag / Fanny pack:** Small pouch worn around the waist for money and documents.

## Most visited places in the Czech Republic
- **Summer:** 
  - **Prague:** Charles Bridge, Prague Castle, Old Town Square.
  - **Český Krumlov:** Historical center and the castle.
  - **Adršpach-Teplice Rocks:** Sandstone rock formations, popular for hiking.
  - **Lipno Dam:** Ideal for swimming, cycling, and summer sports.
- **Winter:**
  - **Krkonoše (Giant Mountains):** Špindlerův Mlýn or Pec pod Sněžkou for skiing and snowboarding.
  - **Šumava:** Popular for cross-country skiing trails.

## Most visited places in the world (From a Czech perspective)
1. **Croatia:** The primary summer destination for Czechs (Makarska, Split, Zadar) due to geographical proximity, affordable prices, and the Adriatic Sea.
2. **Italy:** Popular for both summer (Bibione, Caorle, Rome) and winter (Dolomites - skiing).
3. **Slovakia:** High Tatras (Vysoké Tatry) for hiking and wellness (thermal baths like Tatralandia).
4. **Egypt / Turkey:** Very popular for all-inclusive resort holidays by the sea.
