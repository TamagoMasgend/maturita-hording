# 17 - English and American Literature

## William Shakespeare (1564 – 1616)

- **Origin:** English playwright, poet, and actor. He is widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world's greatest dramatist.
- **Life:** Born and raised in Stratford-upon-Avon. Son of a glover. His family was financially situated. He married Anne Hathaway at 18 and had three children. He moved to London in his early twenties due to his son's death, where he became a successful actor and part-owner of a playing company called the Lord Chamberlain's Men (later the King's Men). They built and performed in the famous **Globe Theatre**.
- **Genres & Output:** He wrote 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and two long narrative poems. His plays are generally divided into three categories: Comedies, Tragedies, and Histories.
- **Notable Works:**
  - _Tragedies:_ Romeo and Juliet, Hamlet, Macbeth, Othello, King Lear. (Dark plays usually involving the death of the main character due to a fatal flaw).
  - _Comedies:_ The Taming of the Shrew, Comedy of errors, As you like it.
  - _Histories:_ King John, Henry IV., Henry V.
- **Details of "Romeo and Juliet":** A tragedy about two young star-crossed lovers from Verona whose families (the Montagues and the Capulets) are bitter enemies. Their secret marriage fails to end the family feud, and a series of tragic misunderstandings leads to both lovers committing suicide, which finally reconciles the grieving families.

## Ernest Hemingway (1899 – 1961)

- **Origin:** American novelist, short-story writer, and journalist. He is a prominent member of the "Lost Generation" (writers who came of age during WWI).
- **Life:** He was born in a state of Illinois, to a doctor and a musician. Spent his childhood in Michigan where he learned to hunt and fish. After high school, instead of going to college he started working as a journalist in Kansas City Star. WWI - he was a ambulance driver for the Red Cross in Italy and was wounded by mortar shells. His life was adventurous. As he visited many countries as a journalist including Spain, Cuba, etc. In the end he shot himself with shotgun due to his mental and physical problems/pains.
- **Writing Style:** Famous for the "Iceberg Theory" – his writing style is very minimalist, direct, and unadorned. Most of the meaning and emotion is hidden beneath the surface of the simple text.
- **Awards:** Won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1954.
- **Notable Works:**
  - _The Old Man and the Sea:_ A short novel about an aging Cuban fisherman, Santiago, who struggles with a giant marlin far out in the Gulf Stream. It explores themes of perseverance, pride, and man vs. nature. (This book won him the Pulitzer Prize).
  - _The Sun also Rises:_ A novel about a group of American and British expatriates in Paris and Spain in the 1920s. It explores themes of post-war disillusionment, love, and loss.
  - _For Whom the Bell Tolls:_ A novel taking place during the Spanish Civil War.
