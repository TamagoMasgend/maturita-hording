# 14 - Prague

## 1) Basic info

Prague is the capital and the largest city of the Czech Republic. It is situated in the centre of Bohemia along the Vltava River. It has a population of approximately 1.3 million people. Prague is the political, cultural, and economic centre of the country. Because of its preserved medieval core and hundreds of spires, it is officially nicknamed "the City of a Hundred Spires" and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site.

## 2) Legend

According to the ancient legend, the founding of Prague was prophesied by a mythical figure, Princess Libuše. Standing on a cliff above the Vltava River, she looked towards the future site of the city and said, "I see a great city whose glory will touch the stars." She ordered a castle to be built where they found a man adjusting the threshold (Czech: práh) of his house, giving the city the name "Praha."

## 3) History

The area around Prague has been inhabited since the Stone Age. The Celtic tribe in Bohemia before being displaced by Germanic tribes, and later by Slavic peoples. Prague Castle was founded in the 9th century by the Přemyslid dynasty, and a marketplace settlement grew on the opposite bank of the Vltava. It became the seat of the Bohemian kings and later the capital of the Holy Roman Empire (under Charles IV in the 14th century, which was Prague's golden age). After the Battle of White Mountain (1620), it fell under Habsburg rule. In 1918, it became the capital of the newly established independent Czechoslovakia. It survived both World Wars mostly physically intact, unlike other European capitals. In 1989, Prague was the centre of the Velvet Revolution, bringing down the communist regime.

## 4) Interesting places

1. **Prague Castle:** It is the largest ancient castle complex in the world. Dominating the city skyline, it has been the seat of Czech kings, emperors, and presidents. It contains St. Vitus Cathedral, the Old Royal Palace, and Golden Lane.
2. **Charles Bridge:** A historic stone bridge that crosses the Vltava River. Built in 1357 under the auspices of King Charles IV, designed by Peter Parler, it is decorated by an alley of 30 statues of saints. It is strictly pedestrian.
3. **Old Town Square with the Astronomical Clock:** The historic heart of the city. It features the famous medieval Astronomical Clock (Orloj) on the Old Town Hall, which displays an animated procession of the Twelve Apostles every hour.
4. **Wenceslas Square:** Less of a square and more of a long boulevard. It is a centre of commerce and culture and has historically been the site of many important public gatherings, protests, and celebrations.
5. **The National Museum (Národní muzeum):** A massive neo-Renaissance building sitting at the top of Wenceslas Square.It houses millions of items across natural history, archaeology, and Czech cultural heritage. The main hall with its grand staircase. After a lengthy renovation, the interior now connects to a modern new building next door through an underground passage.
6. **The National Gallery (Národní galerie):** Not a single building but a network of galleries spread across Prague. They hold the country's most important art collections, ranging from medieval Czech panel paintings to French Impressionists and Czech avant-garde works.
7. **Vyšehrad:** It is tied to the oldest Czech legends, including the story of Princess Libuše and used to be the seat for Czech rulers. Inside the complex sits the Basilica of St. Peter and St. Paul, and the Vyšehrad Cemetery, where many of the greatest Czech composers, writers, and artists are buried.

## 6) Name 5 people connected to Prague (from different eras)

1. **Charles IV (1316–1378):** Holy Roman Emperor who established the University, Charles Bridge, and New Town.
2. **Rudolf II (1552–1612):** Habsburg Emperor who made Prague his capital, attracting alchemists, astronomers (Tycho Brahe), and artists.
3. **Franz Kafka (1883–1924):** World-famous German-speaking Bohemian Jewish novelist and short-story writer born in Prague.
4. **Václav Havel (1936–2011):** Playwright, dissident, and the first President of the independent Czech Republic, strongly connected to the Velvet Revolution in Prague.
5. **Karel Gott (1939–2019):** Extremely famous and successful Czech singer closely associated with the modern cultural life of the city.

## 7) Sport in Prague

1. **epet ARENA (Letná Stadium):** The home stadium of AC Sparta Prague, a major football club.
2. **Fortuna Arena (Eden):** The home stadium of SK Slavia Prague, the other major football club in the famous Prague derby.
3. **O2 Arena:** A massive modern multipurpose indoor arena used for the Ice Hockey World Championships, concerts, and other major sporting events.
