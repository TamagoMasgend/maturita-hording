# 20 - Canada

## 1. Basic facts
Canada is the second-largest country in the world by total area. It is located in the northern half of North America. Its capital is **Ottawa**. It is a bilingual country with two official languages: English and French (primarily spoken in Quebec). The population is relatively small for its massive size, around 40 million. The currency is the Canadian Dollar (CAD).

## 2. Geography
- **Mountains:** The western part of Canada is highly mountainous, dominated by the **Canadian Rockies** (part of the larger Rocky Mountains) and the Coast Mountains in British Columbia. The highest peak is **Mount Logan** (5,959m).
- **Lakes:** Canada has more lakes than the rest of the world combined. Notable ones include the **Great Bear Lake**, **Great Slave Lake**, and it shares four of the five Great Lakes (Superior, Huron, Erie, Ontario) with the USA.
- **Rivers:** The most important river is the **St. Lawrence River**, which links the Great Lakes to the Atlantic Ocean. Another major river is the **Mackenzie River** in the north.
- **Parts / Regions:** 
  - *North:* Covered by arctic tundra and ice (Canadian Arctic Archipelago), virtually uninhabited because of severe cold.
  - *Center / Prairies:* Massive flat lowlands used for agriculture (Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba).
  - *East & West coast:* Heavily forested (taiga/boreal forests) and rocky.

## 3. People
The majority of the population lives within 100 miles (160 km) of the US border because the northern climate is too harsh. Indigenous peoples include First Nations, Inuit (living in the far north), and Métis. Today, Canada is a highly diverse country known for its welcoming immigration policies. About a quarter of the population is French-Canadian.

## 4. History
- Originally inhabited by various Indigenous peoples.
- In the 15th and 16th centuries, British and French expeditions explored and later settled along the Atlantic coast.
- France ceded nearly all of its colonies in North America to Britain in 1763 after the Seven Years' War.
- In 1867, the British North America Act established Canada as a self-governing dominion (confederation).
- Full legal autonomy from the UK was finally achieved in 1982.

## 5. Industry
Canada has a highly developed, mixed economy. It is very rich in natural resources. Its major industries include:
- **Energy:** Heavy extraction of oil and natural gas (particularly from the Athabasca oil sands in Alberta).
- **Mining:** Uranium, gold, diamonds, and potash.
- **Forestry:** One of the world's leading exporters of timber and paper products.
- **Manufacturing & Tech:** Cars, aerospace (Bombardier), and a thriving tech/game-development sector in Toronto and Montreal.

## 6. Government (provinces and territories)
Canada is a parliamentary democracy and a constitutional monarchy (King Charles III is the ceremonial head of state). The head of the government is the Prime Minister.
Canada is composed of **10 provinces** (which have a large degree of autonomy) and **3 territories** (which are managed more directly by the federal government, located in the far north).

## 7. Interesting places
1. **Niagara Falls:** Massive waterfalls situated on the border between the Canadian province of Ontario and the US state of New York. The Canadian side (Horseshoe Falls) is widely considered the more spectacular view.
2. **Banff National Park:** Located in the Rocky Mountains (Alberta), it is Canada's oldest national park. It features stunning alpine landscapes, glaciers, and the beautiful, turquoise-coloured Lake Louise.
3. **The CN Tower:** A massive 553-metre-high concrete communications and observation tower located in downtown Toronto, which held the record for the world's tallest free-standing structure for over 30 years.
