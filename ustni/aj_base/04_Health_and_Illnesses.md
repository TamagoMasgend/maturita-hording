# 04 - Health and Illnesses

## 1) Types of illnesses
Illnesses can be generally divided into two main categories:
**Serious illnesses:** 
- Often life-threatening, requiring long-term medical care, or incurable (mortal).
- **Cancer:** A disease where abnormal cells divide uncontrollably and destroy body tissue. Highly dangerous and requires chemotherapy or surgery.
- **Diabetes:** A chronic disease that occurs when the pancreas does not produce enough insulin, or when the body cannot effectively use the insulin it produces.
- **Heart diseases:** Conditions that involve narrowed or blocked blood vessels that can lead to a heart attack, chest pain, or stroke.
**Common illnesses:**
- Usually easily treatable and not life-threatening.
- **Flu (Influenza):** A viral infection that attacks the respiratory system. Symptoms include fever, chills, muscle aches, and fatigue.
- **Common cold:** Causes a runny nose, sore throat, and a mild cough.

## 4) Medical care in the Czech Republic
- **Level and Quality:** The Czech healthcare system is considered one of the best in Europe. The quality of medical professionals, hospitals, and specialized care is very high.
- **Accessibility:** Medical care is highly accessible to all citizens, with a dense network of general practitioners, specialists, and hospitals.
- **Financing / Insurance:** The system is based on mandatory health insurance. Every citizen, or their employer, must pay monthly health insurance contributions to one of the public health insurance companies (such as VZP). In return, most medical procedures, visits to the doctor, hospital stays, and essential medications are completely free of charge at the point of use. 

## 5) Keeping fit and healthy diet
**General rules for a healthy lifestyle:**
- Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins, minerals, fibre, and lean proteins (meat, fish, eggs).
- Eat at least 5 portions of fruit and vegetables a day.
- Avoid fast food, highly processed foods, excess sugar, and saturated fats.
- Drink plenty of water (at least 2-3 liters a day) and limit alcohol and sugary drinks.
- Engage in regular physical activity (e.g., 30 minutes of walking or exercise daily).
- Ensure sufficient sleep (7-8 hours a night) and manage stress.
