# 08 - Sports and games

## 1. Reasons for sport

1. **Health and Fitness:** Keeps the body fit, builds muscles, and strengthens the immune and cardiovascular systems.
2. **Mental Health:** Relieves stress, clears the mind, and releases endorphins (the "happy hormones").
3. **Socializing:** A great way to meet new people, build teamwork, and spend time with friends.
4. **Entertainment / Career:** It is fun and a popular way to spend free time. For professionals, it is a well-paid career.

## 2. Dividing of sport

- **Indoor sports:** Played inside a building/hall. Examples: Basketball, Gymnastics, Squash.
- **Outdoor sports:** Played outside in the open air. Examples: Football (Soccer), Golf, Cycling.
- **Summer sports:** Played primarily in warm weather or water. Examples: Swimming, Beach Volleyball, Tennis.
- **Winter sports:** Require snow or ice. Examples: Skiing, Ice Hockey, Snowboarding.

1. At our school, we have PE lessons twice a week.
2. In summer, we usually go outside to the athletic track to run (sprints, long distance) or play football.
3. In winter, we stay in the school gym where we do gymnastics, play basketball, volleyball, or floorball. The teacher marks us based on our effort and performance.

## 5. Olympic Games

1. **Origin:** Originated in Ancient Greece in the city of **Olympia** in 776 BC, held to honor the god Zeus. Athletes from Greek city-states competed in events like running, wrestling, boxing, and chariot racing. The games were held every four years. The ancient games were abolished by the Roman Emperor Theodosius I, who considered them a pagan festival.
2. **Resume:** The modern Olympic Games were revived by French educator **Pierre de Coubertin**, who believed in the power of sport to promote peace. The first modern Olympics were held in **Athens in 1896** with 14 nations participating.
3. **Pause:** The Games were cancelled three times during the 20th century because of World War I and World War II.
4. **Symbols:** The Olympic flag has five interlocking rings (blue, yellow, black, green, and red) on a white background, representing the unity of the five inhabited continents. Another symbol is the Olympic Flame, carried by a relay runner from Greece to the host city.
5. **Types:** There are Summer Olympic Games and Winter Olympic Games, rotating every two years (each is held every four years).
6. **Motto:** The Olympic motto is "Citius, Altius, Fortius" (Faster, Higher, Stronger).

## 6. Rules of 1 game (Football / Soccer)

- Football is played on a rectangular grass pitch.
- Two teams play against each other. Each team has 11 players on the field, including one goalkeeper.
- The objective is to score goals by getting the ball into the opponent's net.
- Players are not allowed to touch the ball with their hands or arms (except for the goalkeeper within the penalty area).
- A standard match lasts 90 minutes, divided into two 45-minute halves with a 15-minute half-time break.
- The referee enforces the rules and can give yellow cards (warnings) or red cards (ejection from the game) for serious fouls.
