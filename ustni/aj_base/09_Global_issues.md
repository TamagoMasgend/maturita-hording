# 09 - Global issues

## 1) Definition
A global issue is a problem or risk that negatively affects the entire world or a significant portion of it. These issues cannot be solved by a single country and require international cooperation, political agreements, and global awareness to be resolved.

## 2) Name 8 global problems
1. Climate Change and Global Warming
2. Poverty and Starvation (Hunger)
3. Water Scarcity and Pollution
4. Deforestation
5. Overpopulation
6. Wars and Armed Conflicts
7. Epidemics and Pandemics (Diseases like COVID-19 or HIV/AIDS)
8. Racism and Discrimination

## 3) Choose 5 global problems and describe them in detail

### 1. Climate Change (Global Warming)
- **Where:** Worldwide, particularly affecting coastal areas (rising sea levels) and polar regions (melting ice caps).
- **How many people affected:** Billions, practically the whole planet's ecosystem.
- **Why it exists / What it caused:** Caused by greenhouse gases (CO2) from burning fossil fuels (coal, oil) and deforestation. It causes extreme weather (droughts, floods), rising temperatures, and destroys habitats.
- **Possible solution:** Transitioning to renewable energy (solar, wind), planting trees, reducing emissions.
- **Green Deal relevance:** The European Green Deal aims to make Europe climate-neutral by 2050 by reducing greenhouse gas emissions and advancing green technologies.

### 2. Poverty and Starvation
- **Where:** Mostly in developing countries in Africa, parts of Asia, and South America.
- **How many people affected:** Over 700 million people live in extreme poverty.
- **Why it exists / What it caused:** Caused by economic instability, political corruption, lack of education, and natural disasters. It leads to malnutrition, high child mortality, and lack of basic hygiene.
- **Possible solution:** Improving access to education, fairer international trade, and humanitarian food aid.
- **Organization that helps:** UNICEF, World Food Programme.

### 3. Deforestation
- **Where:** Heavily impacts the Amazon Rainforest in Brazil, Indonesia, and Central Africa.
- **How many people affected:** Millions of indigenous people directly, and the entire globe indirectly.
- **Why it exists / What it caused:** Forests are cut down or burned to make space for agriculture (soy, palm oil, cattle) and logging. It destroys biodiversity, kills animal species, and rapidly worsens global warming because trees absorb CO2.
- **Possible solution:** Stricter logging laws, boycotting products with unsustainable palm oil, mass tree-planting campaigns.
- **Organization that helps:** Greenpeace, WWF.

### 4. Water Scarcity and Pollution
- **Where:** Sub-Saharan Africa, Middle East, and parts of Asia.
- **How many people affected:** Currently, around 2 billion people lack access to safely managed drinking water.
- **Why it exists / What it caused:** Caused by global warming drying up rivers, overpopulation, and industrial/agricultural waste polluting freshwater sources (plastic and chemicals in oceans). Causes deadly diseases (cholera).
- **Possible solution:** Desalination technologies, stricter waste disposal laws, and saving water in households.
- **Organization that helps:** Water.org, WHO.

### 5. Wars and Armed Conflicts
- **Where:** Currently in places like Ukraine, the Middle East (Syria, Gaza), and various African nations.
- **How many people affected:** Tens of millions (refugees and casualties).
- **Why it exists / What it caused:** Disputes over territory, resources, religion, or political power. It causes massive loss of life, destruction of infrastructure, and forces millions of people to flee their homes as refugees.
- **Possible solution:** Diplomatic negotiations, international peace treaties, and sanctions.
- **Organization that helps:** UN (United Nations), Red Cross.

## 4) Name 10 Organizations and their purpose
1. **UN (United Nations):** Maintains international peace and security, develops friendly relations among nations.
2. **NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization):** A military alliance defending its member states against external threats.
3. **EU (European Union):** Political and economic union of European states promoting free trade and movement.
4. **WHO (World Health Organization):** Directs international health, fights diseases, and manages global health crises.
5. **UNICEF (United Nations Children's Fund):** Provides humanitarian and developmental aid to children worldwide.
6. **WWF (World Wide Fund for Nature):** Dedicated to environmental conservation and protecting endangered animals.
7. **Greenpeace:** An NGO that campaigns against environmental degradation, deforestation, and climate change.
8. **Amnesty International:** Fights for human rights and campaigns against torture, the death penalty, and political imprisonment.
9. **Red Cross / Red Crescent:** Provides emergency assistance, disaster relief, and medical care in war zones.
10. **UNESCO (UN Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization):** Promotes world peace and security through international cooperation in education, sciences, and culture (protects World Heritage Sites).
